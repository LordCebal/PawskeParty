Early arrival city walk
If you are in town extra early you might want to check out some of the sights Oslo has to offer. On Wednesday April 5th we invite you to walk around Oslo for a few hours with us. The walk begins at 12:00 at the tiger statue at Jernbanetorget. From there we set out and will among other things stop by the Opera, Akershus Fortress, the Royal Palace, and the parliament. The trip will end at approximately 15:00 at Outland pop culture store.
This trip is free for all and does not require a ticket.
Pre-party Bowling
When: April 5th at 17:00
Arriving early? Join the pre-party bowling!
We’re warming up to the big party with some fursuit bowling at Veitvet Bowling just outside downtown Oslo. We have reserved lanes and a changing room from 17-19 on Wednesday April 5th and are gearing up for two hours of strikes and gutter balls.
Drinks and food can be purchased at the bowling
Veitvet is easily reached by Metro line 5 Vestli, and bus 31 Grorud T.
In 2013 Veitvet bowling was the location for a fursuit bowling filmed by the state broadcaster NRK for an article about furries.
Tickets cost 180 NOK and are bought together with your entry ticket. Available until midnight April 2nd.
Post-Party Spa Day
When: April 7th at 12:30
After a long night of dancing and partying, it’s nice to relax and recuperate. At The Well warm pools, jacuzzis, steam bath and other wellness attractions awaits your sore muscles and aching heads.
The Well is the biggest spa center in the Nordic region and offers a wide array of things to do, including various wellness rituals that are included in the ticket. On their website you can also add up-charges like a massage or various skin treatments. We recommend you check out their website for all they have to offer.
At The Well you are free to choose between being naked and wearing swimming clothes. You are only allowed to wear The Well’s own brand that can be bought in the reception upon arrival. See designs and prices on their website. Phones are not allowed past the locker room.
The Well is located a bit outside of Oslo and is accessed by car or bus. There will not be an organized travel out to the center, but it is easy to find. The Well is located at Kongeveien 65, 1412 Sofiemyr and has it’s own parking lot. From Jernbanetorget in Oslo you can take bus 81 towards Myrvoll Stasjon to Granholtet. Find your best route on EnTur or through Google.
Time table:
12:20 – Gathering by the front entrance (towards the parking lot)
12:30 – Entrance to the spa center
15:00 – Lunch. Pizza and salad sharing table
22:00 – Closing time
You are free to leave whenever you feel like it, but you must be there by 12:20 to get in. Any late admissions will be at the discretion of the front desk. Due to their no phones policy you will not be able to contact us after we have entered.
The price for the Post-Party Spa Day is 840 NOK. This includes entrance, a bottle of water, lunch, and the use of 1 bathrobe, 2 towels and 1 pair of slippers.
Other things to do
Pawske Party is just the beginning of the Easter holiday.
What are you gonna do with all those other days?
Fear not, Oslo has a lot to offer. From the new National Museum and the controversial Munch Museum, to historical sites like Middelalderparken and Akershus Fortress you can easily spend days in the worlds of art and history.
From Frognerparken to the Opera, Oslo is a destination for design and architecture. You can have a great day just wandering the streets enjoying the sights.
While not recognized on the same level as Copenhagen, Oslo still has a lot to offer on the restaurant front. From our 3 star Michelin restaurant Maaemo, to street food halls and waffle stands there is something for everyone. Fancy a cocktail after dinner? Oslo houses some world class bars, including the legendary Himkok.
On top of all there is to see and do within the city, Oslo are surrounded by forest with excellent hiking opportunities, and nearby several interesting places like Drøbak, Fredrikstad and Halden.
Want to know more check out Visit Oslo, Visit Drøbak and Visit Norway.
